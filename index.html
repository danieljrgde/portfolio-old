Studies

[2022] Effects of Social Media Bots on the Crypto Market

In this research project, the impact of social media bots on the crypto market was investigated through the usage of AI and statistical analysis. A custom data collection script was developed to gather 6M+ market-related social media posts, along with stock price data for the top 50 cryptocurrencies by market cap. Users were classified into two categories (bot or human) based on the content of their posts using an anomaly detection architecture that leverages a Variational Autoencoder and the k-Nearest Neighbors algorithm, taking into account the unbalanced nature of the data. Sentiment analysis was conducted on the social media content using BERT embeddings as input for the neural network, enabling the classification of the emotional tone of social media posts. A custom engagement metric based on the content's metadata was proposed, and the correlation between this engagement metric and the asset's price fluctuations was analyzed.

Natural Language Processing (NLP) Sentiment Analysis Anomaly Detection Variational Autoencoder Deep Learning Statistical Analysis k-Nearest Neighbors (kNN) Hypothesis Testing

[2021] How User Information Drives Amazon's Products Recommendations

This research project investigated how Amazon personalizes product recommendations and pricing based on user information. We developed a data collection system that simulated different user personas by manipulating browser cookies and history, then collected search results of 60k+ products from Amazon. Using statistical measures like the Jaccard Similarity Index and Kendall's τ coefficient, we analyzed the collected data for evidence of price steering and discrimination. Our analysis revealed strong indications of price steering but limited evidence of price discrimination. To examine Amazon's buy-box recommendations, we implemented a Random Forest classifier that achieved 80% accuracy in predicting the recommended seller, with product price and seller metrics as key features. The project combined web scraping techniques, statistical analysis, and machine learning to gain insights into e-commerce personalization strategies.

Random Forest Classifier Feature Importance Similarity Metrics Statistical Analysis Web Scraping Recommender Systems Explainable AI (XAI)

[2021] Effects of the Interest Rate on the Brazilian Stock Market

This project investigated the impact of interest rates on various sectors of the Brazilian stock market using time series analysis. We developed a custom web scraping application to collect historical stock index data from B3 (the Brazilian stock exchange) and leveraged government APIs to gather macroeconomic indicators. The core of our analysis employed SARIMAX (Seasonal Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average with Exogenous variables) models to account for the non-stationary nature of financial time series, including trends and seasonality. We compared these results against traditional OLS (Ordinary Least Squares) regression to highlight the importance of considering time-dependent structures in financial data. The SARIMAX models were optimized using cross-validation and the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) to select the best hyperparameters. Our analysis revealed sector-specific sensitivities to interest rate changes, with real estate and consumer goods showing the most significant responses, while providing insights into the complex dynamics of Brazil's financial markets.

Time Series Analysis SARIMAX Hyperparameter Optimization Web Scraping Feature Engineering Autocorrelation Cross-validation Explainable AI (XAI)

[2021] Analysis of Economic Incentives of Yelp's Fake Reviews for Parisian Restaurants

This research project analyzed restaurant competition and review fraud on Yelp in Paris using data science and natural language processing techniques. We developed custom web scraping tools and utilized Yelp's API to collect data on over 13k restaurants, including details on 250k reviews, ratings, and geographical information. Using statistical analysis and regression models, we examined the economic incentives behind fake reviews, finding that lower-priced and lower-rated restaurants tended to have slightly higher percentages of fraudulent reviews. We also employed sentiment analysis and polarity detection on review text to differentiate characteristics of fake versus genuine reviews. Our analysis leveraged Python for data collection and processing, along with libraries like pandas for data manipulation and scikit-learn for machine learning tasks. The project provided insights into the dynamics of online review platforms and the factors influencing review fraud in the restaurant industry.
